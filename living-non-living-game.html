A wool sweater

Wool comes from a living sheep. The sheep is sheared — not harmed. Is the sweater living, dead, or non-living?

Non-living — The wool was part of a living sheep, but it was processed (sheared, spun, and knitted into fabric). That process changes its category to non-living.

A caterpillar inside a chrysalis

It is hanging from a branch. It is not moving. It looks like a dry shell. Is it living, dead, or non-living?

Living — Inside the chrysalis, the caterpillar is transforming into a butterfly. It is still using energy and developing. Not moving does not mean not alive!

A shark tooth on the beach

Sharks naturally shed thousands of teeth in their lifetime. The shark is still alive somewhere in the ocean. Is the tooth living, dead, or non-living?

Dead (once living) — The tooth was a living part of a living shark. Once separated from the body, it no longer carries out life processes. It is classified as dead — once living.

Coral in the ocean

It looks like a colorful rock on the ocean floor. It does not have legs or eyes. It grows very, very slowly. Is it living, dead, or non-living?

Living — Coral is made up of tiny living animals called polyps. It grows, reproduces, responds to its environment, and uses energy. Looks can be deceiving!

A glass of orange juice

It was made by squeezing oranges that grew on a tree. Is the orange juice living, dead, or non-living?

Non-living — Oranges came from a living tree, but squeezing them into juice transforms the material into something new. The juice is now non-living.
